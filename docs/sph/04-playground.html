Part 4 of Smooth Particle Hydrodynamics

Everything from the previous articles comes together here. You have particles governed by density, pressure, and viscosity. You have spatial hashing for fast neighbor lookups. And you have material models that change how particles behave.

Pick a material, place emitters and obstacles, paint force fields, and watch particles interact. The toolbar gives you everything you need to build small scenes. Try dropping water onto an obstacle, or filling a space with jelly and poking it.

Playground. Click the canvas to place emitters (drag to set direction), obstacles, or force fields. Particles are colored by speed: dark blue is slow, lighter blue is fast. Switch materials to change the physics.

The sandbox measures frame time and displays the current FPS alongside the particle count. If the simulation struggles to keep up, you can reduce the load by erasing emitters or pressing Reset. The simulation targets 60 fps and caps particles at 500 to stay smooth on most devices.

Everything here runs on a single thread using Canvas 2D. Spatial hashing keeps the neighbor search fast, and substeps keep the integration stable. The same ideas scale to GPU compute shaders as shown in Part 2 if you need thousands of particles.
